Mutiny of 1857: India's First War of Independence

The Indian Rebellion of 1857, known as the "First War of Independence, " is a crucial chapter in India's history. A notable figure in this historic event was Mangal Pandey, whose life story serves as a powerful source of motivation. Stationed in Barrackpore in 1857, Mangal Pandey refused to use cartridges greased with animal fat, a practice that deply offended both Hindus and Muslims. His principled stand against this injustice became a catalyst for the rebellion.
